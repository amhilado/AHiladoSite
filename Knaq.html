Knaq App

Knaq is an app that provides Content Creators with more ownership of their content and instantly provides larger payouts compared to platforms such as Twitch or Youtube, where they take a large percentage of what the Creator earns. This is done through the efficiency of BSV Bitcoin since it has low transaction/gas fees.

For this project, I was the main UX/UI designer working directly with the CEO of Knaq. The main features that we wanted to focus on building out were the tipping/subscriptions, and the in-app wallet. The users would be able to earn feathers, which is the in-app currency, and convert the feathers into cryptocurrency so they can instantly deposit the money they made into their crypto wallet.

Defining the Problem

Content creators put a lot of time and effort into creating, but most platforms take a large percentage of the profit (Ex: Twitch takes 50% of profits gained from bits and subscriptions, Facebook and Instagram take 55% of ad revenue), and require certain prerequisites for creators to start earning money on their platform. This revenue system can drive away and discourage smaller content creators from even creating in the first place.

Challenges:

  • Startup was still in early stages of development
  • Lack of real user data due to time and budgetary constraints
  • Existing UI needed an overhaul

Who are we designing for?

Although we didn't have any real users to help us conduct research, we were able to identify and define the potential users we were building the app for.

Competitive Analysis

Looking at other content creations platforms that pay creators, most of them require creators to meet a specific set of requirements (certain number of followers/subscribers, average view counts/likes, etc.) before they can start earning money. Once the creator does start earning money, they receive their payment at specific periods, whether it be monthly, or reaching a certain revenue threshold. None of the platforms allow creators to instantly deposit their revenue into their account.

Feature Prioritization

Features were prioritized based on value provided to the user. In the case with Knaq, giving users the ability to deposit any money earned directly into their crypto wallet without deducting any transaction fees was one of the main features we wanted to focus on.

Solution

In order to provide creators with more control over their content and the full revenue they have earned through tips and subscriptions, we decided to integrate an in-app wallet. The in-app wallet will allow creators to deposit their revenue earned into their crypto wallet without having to worry about any transaction fees thanks to the low gas fees of BSV Bitcoin.

Visual Design

The in-app wallet was designed to be streamlined and straightforward to make it easier for the user to deposit, withdraw, and send their currency. We narrowed down the number of steps it takes to move currency between the in-app wallet and the userâ€™s crypto wallet in order to make the process more smooth.

As for the tipping/subscription features, we wanted to make it easy and straight-forward to use while maintaining an aesthetically pleasing experience. We simplified the color palette and made the call to action buttons the secondary color to provide contrast against the white background.

Future Considerations

Going forward with this project, I would like to conduct more through research with our user base to gain a better understanding of what current features of the app work well, and what features can be improved. This will be done through more prototyping, user testing, user interviews, and empathy mapping.
